Job & Internship Search

Finding the right job or internship takes planning, preparation, and the right tools. Use this page to learn how to structure your search, discover where to find opportunities, and access resources to make the process easier and more effective.

For a more complete and detailed set of job and internship search resources—including workshop slides, handouts, and planning templates—please visit our Career Exploration and Job/Internship Search module.

1) How to Plan Your Job/Internship Search

Start with a strategy. Define your goals, map a timeline, prepare materials, and track your progress. Focus beats volume—target roles, industries, and locations that fit your skills and values.

Core Steps

  1. Set your direction: role(s), industry, location, and constraints (e.g., timeline, visa).
  2. Build your targets: make a company list (use a LAMP list if helpful) and prioritize outreach.
  3. Ready your materials: resume, LinkedIn, portfolio/GitHub, and a 30-second pitch.
  4. Schedule your search: block weekly time for applications + networking.
  5. Track everything: keep a spreadsheet or use CareerLink to log postings, contacts, and follow-ups.
  6. Iterate weekly: review results, refine targets, and ask a CDO coach for feedback.

2) Where to Find Jobs & Internships

Combine UMSI-exclusive postings with broader platforms and industry-specific boards. Don’t forget on-campus roles and micro-internships to build experience. For more information, visit our Searching for Job and/or Internship Postings canvas page.

UMSI / U-M Platforms

  • UMSI CareerLink – exclusive postings, employer events, and on-campus recruiting.
  • Handshake – U-M student jobs/internships across campus and beyond.
  • U-M Careers – The official site for U-M student employment, offering roles across departments, research labs, and administrative offices.
  • Student Employment Site, ITS Student Roles, CAI Student Roles

Large Job Boards

  • LinkedIn Jobs
  • Indeed
  • Google Jobs
  • WayUp

Industry-Specific Boards

  • Wellfound (startups), Built In (tech hubs)
  • Idealist (non-profits, social impact)
  • Dice (tech/engineering)
  • Archives Gig (archives), USA Jobs (government)
  • TeamworkOnline (sports & entertainment)

Short-Term / Part-Time

Many UMSI students pursue part-time work during their studies. Opportunities can be competitive, especially on campus, so start early and use multiple resources. Here are the main platforms to explore:

  • Student Employment Site – Central hub for Work-Study and non-Work-Study positions. Jobs are updated continuously.
  • UMSI CareerLink – Search current postings, explore past postings under “Expired Jobs,” and set saved searches to get email alerts.
  • UM Library Jobs – Hourly roles across U-M libraries. Consider contacting specific libraries or subject specialists directly for opportunities.
  • Graduate Student Positions (GSI, GSRA, GSSA) – Teaching, research, and staff assistantships. Some positions are competitive and may not be widely posted—network with faculty for leads.
  • U-M Careers – Search for part-time and contract work across the university.
For more information, please visit our Searching for Part-Time and/or On-Campus Jobs handout.

If you have an immediate need or you have any trouble accessing any of these links or resources, please email 539Instructors@umich.edu

3) Job Hunting for International Students

International students can absolutely succeed in the U.S. market—just pair a strong search plan with visa awareness and targeted employers. Use the resources below and meet with a CDO coach and the U-M International Center to align strategy with work authorization.

If you are new in job/internship searching, please go through our [Handout] International Internships & Employment Resources first to understand the overall process.

Key Resources

  • Interstride – employer insights, visa guidance, and global job postings (log in with U-M).
  • U-M International Center – CPT/OPT workshops, travel & work rules, advising.
  • GoinGlobal – country guides, application norms, and H-1B employer data.
  • UMSI Internship Grant – funding support for qualifying international internships.
  • M-Compass – international internship programs and funding opportunities.
  • Third-party program providers (evaluate fit/quality): CRCC Asia, CEA, ISA, Sage Corps, CIEE, etc.

What to Watch

  • CPT/OPT timing: confirm eligibility windows and course requirements before accepting an offer.
  • Sponsorship signals: check H-1B history (e.g., employer databases/Interstride) and job descriptions for sponsorship language.
  • Application materials: tailor for the U.S. market (impact-focused resume, concise cover emails) or adjust to country-specific norms for global searches.
  • Networking first: warm outreach to alumni and affinity communities often beats cold applications.
  • Apply early for funding: UMSI and external grants (e.g., Rotary, Fulbright, CAORC) are competitive.

Tip: Keep a simple tracker for applications, sponsorship status, CPT/OPT notes, and follow-ups. Bring it to coaching appointments so your coach can help you prioritize next steps.
